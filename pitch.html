01 The gap

RL has math and code.
It doesn't have business.

Frontier RL environments reward provable answers — theorems, unit tests, won games. But the most valuable agentic skill of all — running a business: finding what a market wants, building it, and pricing it — has no verifiable gym. Grading "good strategy" with an LLM judge is noisy and gameable.

💡 Profit is the perfect reward. It's a single number a simulator can compute exactly — impossible to fake, and it only goes up if the agent actually understands the market.

🎯 Economically real

The task is the job — discovery, allocation, and pricing under uncertainty — not a synthetic puzzle.

🔒 Verifiable

Reward is computed by a deterministic market simulation. No judge to persuade, nothing to game.

🧭 Open-ended

Hidden latent structure forces genuine explore-then-exploit — exactly what makes a task RL-native.

02 The environment

Not a demand curve —
a market that behaves like a real one.

An agent — or a team — operates a SaaS against up to 500,000 heterogeneous customers, hidden inside a fresh randomized market each episode. It runs experiments to reverse-engineer it over ~16 rounds.

Hidden world
up to 500,000 users from hidden personas — correlated pains, willingness-to-pay, preferred channel, churn.
→
Agent acts
6 MCP tools: probe, build, price, run campaigns — within a round, then commit.
→
Market sim
A deterministic funnel runs: reach → try → buy → subscribe → churn.
→
Reward
Per-target diagnostics + profit. Reward = profit ÷ a theoretical ceiling. Repeat.

👥 Users — a population, not a curve

Every customer is unique — hidden per-user dimensions:

their pains willingness-to-pay price elasticity reachable channel quality bar loyalty / churn + per-user noise

🛠️ Actions — an operator's toolkit, not a price dial

Each round, the agent can:

run user research target pains write ad copy allocate budget set pricing build features via specs manage retention
03 / 08
03 Why it's a real benchmark

Execution-based reward.
Nothing to fake.

⚖️ No LLM judge

The sim computes profit from a up to 500,000-user funnel. The agent can't argue its way to a score.

📐 Honest [0,1]

reward = profit ÷ a theoretical ceiling — an optimistic true upper bound, not a policy. No clipping, no "beat the oracle."

🎲 Domain-randomized

New hidden world every episode. Generalization is measured on held-out seeds — not a within-episode split.

🧩 Matched-quad design

Every latent has an action, an observation, and a reward channel — so every skill is both learnable and graded.

06 Two modes — solo founder or full company

Solo founder, or a whole company.

The hard part of a company isn't one genius — it's a team that coordinates. Run SaaSBench with one agent, or as a team of role-agents that each see only their slice and must communicate to win — the same execution-based profit reward drives both modes.

🛠️ Builder

Picks & specs features. Knows what it built — and must tell the team.

📣 Marketer

Targets pains × channels, writes the ad copy, spends the budget.

🏷️ Pricer

Sets price for conversion vs churn — the lifetime-value lever.

🧭 Coordinator

Allocates budget, sets direction, commits the round.

📉 A coordination tax — new skill, same rigor

Each role sees a partial view. The Marketer must learn from the Builder's message which feature exists — or it burns budget promoting nothing. We grade team profit vs a single-agent, full-info oracle: the gap is the cost of poor coordination. Still execution-based, still ungameable.

🧠 One checkpoint, four roles

Not four models — one shared policy, role-conditioned by prompt. Every role-turn shares the team's profit reward; GRPO normalizes within each world's rollout group. Parameter sharing — the only sane shape for cooperative LLM agents.
